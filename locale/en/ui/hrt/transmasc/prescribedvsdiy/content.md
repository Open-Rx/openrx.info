# Transmasculine Guide: Prescribed vs DIY
 
### Legal Considerations:

The legal risk of possession of testosterone is very low. Although technically illegal in countries like the US, authorities rarely prosecute individuals for personal use. Legal issues might arise if testosterone is discovered during an unrelated search.

In countries like Canada and the UK, personal possession of testosterone is legal, only distribution or importation (ordering it from outside the country) is prohibited.

Ordering internationally poses a slight risk, as customs may confiscate the package. However, legal penalties are very rare. Typically, you’ll just receive a seizure notice. To avoid this, try to order from domestic sources, which are generally very common to find, and not subject to customs checks.

### Prescribed HRT:

Informed consent ensures you understand all the information about a medical treatment and accept the risks involved.

Informed consent in the context of transmasc HRT means that the the effects of testosterone (including risks and side effects) are explained to you by a medical professional. You can then agree or refuse starting HRT. This can be an easy and affordable way of starting testosterone legally in some countries.

Under an informed consent model, a person is able to start HRT very quickly (within days or weeks of the first appointment) after they agree to receiving HRT treatment.

Most of these services are only available to people who are above the age of 18 and are residing in the USA, Europe/UK, or Australia. A few US-based services may allow for those under 18 to obtain care, with parental consent, depending on state.

See this page for a full list for all current informed consent resources: [Informed Consent and Telehealth](https://diyhrt.info/resources/telehealth/).

To save on prescribed testosterone in the USA, try [goodrx.com](https://goodrx.com/) for free coupons to use at participating pharmacies. There are no strings attached to this service! [Cost Plus Drugs](https://costplusdrugs.com/medications/) is also a good source for affordable medications delivered to you by mail if you have a doctor’s prescription. CostPlusDrugs and GoodRX don’t prescribe medication, they just allow you to obtain medication that has already been prescribed to you at a lower cost.

### DIY HRT:

If you don’t want to be on waitlists, have to prove your transness while allowing your body to feminize, or are unable to get T legally, then you may have to order it online. You may need to learn how to buy Bitcoin. There are many guides to buying Bitcoin if you look online. (If you are unable to, get a trusted friend to purchase the Bitcoin for you, or go to your local BTC ATM if your country has any.) Some sites also offer payment in methods such as Zelle, Bank Transfer, Western Union and MoneyGram. (Keep in mind that these methods are often subject to minimum order requirements, unlike Bitcoin.)

If you choose to order testosterone without a prescription, it will be illegal in most countries (although the risk of a domestic order being seized, let alone any person in possession of anabolic steroids for personal use being prosecuted, is unheard of). Testosterone is legal to possess without a prescription in places like the UK and Canada (sale of testosterone without a proper license is illegal, however).
