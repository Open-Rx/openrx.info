## Sending to a Vendor

This is where funds get lost. Crypto transactions are final — there's no reversal, no dispute, no way to get it back if something goes wrong.

### Get the exact address and network from your vendor

Your vendor needs to tell you two things: the wallet address and which network to send on. These are not interchangeable.

Bitcoin addresses only work for BTC. For stablecoins like USDT or USDC, the same address format might exist on multiple networks (Ethereum/ERC-20, Tron/TRC-20, Solana, etc.) and they don't cross over. Sending USDT on the wrong network to a vendor expecting a different network means the money is gone.

If the vendor gives you a QR code, scan it, but still verify the address text and the network before sending.

### Before you hit send

- Copy and paste the address — don't type it manually
- Check the first and last 4 characters of the pasted address against what the vendor gave you
- Confirm you're sending the right coin (BTC, USDT, USDC, etc.)
- Confirm the network matches what the vendor expects
- Check the amount one more time after fees are shown
- If the amount is large, consider sending a small test amount first and confirming receipt before sending the rest
