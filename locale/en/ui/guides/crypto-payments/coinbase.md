## Coinbase

Coinbase is one of the larger US exchanges. It supports BTC, USDC, USDT, and most other common coins. You'll need to go through identity verification before you can buy anything.

### Setting up an account

Create an account with your email, verify it, and complete identity verification — name, address, date of birth, and government ID. Set up two-factor authentication while you're at it.

### Linking a payment method

In settings, go to Payment methods and add a bank account (ACH) or debit card. ACH takes a few days to verify; debit cards are instant. Credit cards generally aren't supported for purchases.

### Buying crypto

Click Buy/Sell at the top (desktop) or tap Buy (mobile). Select the coin, enter the amount, pick your payment method, and preview the order. Review the fees and total before you confirm.

### Sending to a vendor

Click Send/Receive and then Send (desktop), or tap Send (mobile). Pick the asset, then pay attention to the network selection — this is where mistakes happen. For stablecoins especially, the vendor needs to tell you which network they're on (ERC-20, TRC-20, etc.). Sending on the wrong chain almost always means permanent loss of funds.

Paste the vendor's wallet address, verify the first and last four characters, enter the amount, review, and send.
