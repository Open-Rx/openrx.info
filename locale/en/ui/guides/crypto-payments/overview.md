## Paying with Crypto: Why Vendors Don't Accept Fiat

Gray-market vendors for HRT and GLP-1 medications almost universally require cryptocurrency. It's not preference — it's the only payment method that actually works for them.

### Why not cards or PayPal?

Payment processors like Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, and Stripe prohibit transactions for pharmaceuticals sold outside licensed channels. Vendor accounts that process these payments get terminated and the transactions get reversed. Vendors have no real choice.

Cryptocurrency doesn't have that problem. Transactions are peer-to-peer, irreversible, and don't need approval from any payment network.

### Privacy: this actually matters

Not all crypto is equally private, and for gray-market purchases that distinction is worth understanding.

Bitcoin and most other coins are *pseudonymous*. Your name isn't on the transaction, but every transaction is permanently recorded on a public blockchain. Exchanges are required to collect your identity (KYC), and chain analysis firms can and do trace funds from exchanges back through transaction history. Governments subpoena this data regularly.

Monero is genuinely different. Ring signatures, stealth addresses, and RingCT are built into the base protocol, not added on top. Sender, recipient, and amount are all cryptographically hidden by default. There's no public ledger to analyze. This isn't a workaround or a mixing service; it's how the coin works.

| Cryptocurrency | Privacy | Ease of purchase |
|---|---|---|
| Monero (XMR) | Excellent | Moderate (harder to buy) |
| Bitcoin (BTC) | Poor | Easy |
| USDT / USDC | Poor | Easy |
| ETH | Poor | Easy |

### What we recommend

Use Monero if you can. It takes more effort to get than Bitcoin but it's the only mainstream option that provides real privacy. The [Monero guide](/guides/crypto-payments/monero) covers how to get started.

If Monero isn't working for you, Bitcoin via Cash App or Coinbase is the most common fallback. Just go in with eyes open about the tradeoffs.

### What most vendors accept

BTC and USDT are almost universal. Many gray-market vendors accept XMR and some actually prefer it or offer a small discount for it. A few accept PayPal or cards directly, though this is less common and riskier for them.
