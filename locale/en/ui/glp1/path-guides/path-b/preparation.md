## Preparation & Use — Drawing a Single-Dose Vial

Work slowly, use sterile supplies, and measure accurately.

**Step 1: Set up**

Wash hands, arrange vial/syringe/swabs/sharps container. Allow refrigerated vials to warm for 15–30 minutes at room temperature before drawing.

**Step 2: Disinfect the vial**

Remove the plastic cap. Scrub the rubber stopper with a fresh alcohol swab for at least 10 seconds. Let it air-dry. Do not touch the stopper after cleaning.

**Step 3: Ready the syringe**

Open the sterile syringe package. Attach the needle if it comes separately. Remove the needle cap.

**Step 4: Draw in air**

Pull the plunger back to your target volume (e.g., 0.5 mL).

**Step 5: Pressurize the vial**

Insert the needle through the center of the rubber stopper. Inject the air into the vial. Keep the needle in place.

**Step 6: Withdraw the medication**

Invert the vial and syringe together. Keep the needle tip below the liquid surface. Pull the plunger past your target line so the syringe holds a little extra solution.

**Step 7: Clear bubbles & set the dose**

Flick the barrel so bubbles rise to the needle hub. Slowly push the plunger until the stopper aligns exactly with your dose line.

**Step 8: Remove the syringe**

Extract the needle from the stopper. Place the syringe on a clean surface and proceed to injection.
