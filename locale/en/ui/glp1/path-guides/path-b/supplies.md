## Supplies Needed & Sourcing (Path B)

- **Medication vial** — An authentic, single-dose tirzepatide vial in the strength you need. Always confirm the serial code before use.
- **Insulin syringes** — Sterile U-100 syringes. A 1 mL barrel with 0.01 mL markings gives the best accuracy. Use a brand-new syringe for every injection; never reuse.
- **Needles (if separate)** — Sterile, subcutaneous gauge 29 G–32 G, length 5/16-inch or ½-inch, compatible with your syringe. One new needle per shot.
- **Alcohol swabs** — Individually wrapped, sterile pads for wiping the vial stopper and skin.
- **Gauze or cotton** — Optional, to press on the site for a few seconds after injecting.
- **Sharps container** — Puncture-resistant, FDA-cleared container for used syringes, needles, and empty vials.

**Where to buy:** Vials through a trusted gray-market supplier (authenticate before use). Ancillary supplies at local pharmacies or reputable online medical-supply stores. See the [Ancillary Supplies guide](/glp-1/sourcing/ancillary-supplies).

Follow the [Safe Disposal Guide](/glp-1/safe-disposal) for all sharps and empty vials.
