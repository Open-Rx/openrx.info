## Using Ready-to-Use Options (Pen, Syringe, Tablet)

Path A uses GLP-1 RA medication in formats that need almost no preparation: single-use pens (for example, **Tizaro**), pre-filled syringes (**Semaglo OB**), or oral tablets (**Rybelsus®**). These items are often imported through gray-market channels.

### Specific Risks & Considerations

- **Shipping and customs:** Parcels can be delayed, lost, or seized. For personal-use quantities, seizure is highly unlikely.
- **Limited support:** If the pen or syringe malfunctions, getting a replacement or refund is unlikely.
- **Lack of medical oversight:** Using any GLP-1 without a clinician means side effects and drug interactions may go unnoticed.

Always inspect products carefully upon receipt. Check packaging for damage, verify the product name and dose, and check the expiry date. For injectables, visually examine the liquid before each use — it should typically be clear and colorless (Tizaro may be slightly yellow). Discard if cloudy, discolored, contains particles, or if the device appears damaged or expired. Never use medication suspected of having been frozen.
