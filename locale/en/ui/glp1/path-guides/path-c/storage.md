## Storage (Reconstituted Vial)

Proper storage after mixing preserves potency and limits bacterial growth (sterility is never guaranteed).

- **Refrigeration:** Store the vial upright in a refrigerator between 2 °C and 8 °C (36 °F to 46 °F). Keep it toward the back where temperatures stay steady.
- **Do not freeze:** Freezing damages peptide molecules and makes the solution ineffective.
- **Protect from light:** Keep the vial in its original box or wrap it in aluminium foil.
- **Label the vial:** Record the date mixed, final concentration, and calculated expiration date.
- **Shelf life:** Most users limit storage to **28 days** when mixed with BAC water and refrigerated. Inspect the solution before every dose — discard if cloudy, discolored, or if particles appear.
- **Single-use if mixed with plain sterile water:** Without preservative, treat the vial as single use and discard any leftover solution immediately after the first withdrawal.

### Unmixed Powder Storage

Store lyophilized (freeze-dried) peptide powder in the freezer at -4 °F (-20 °C) or colder. Powder can last 1–2 years frozen. Let it reach room temperature before opening.

### Traveling with Reconstituted Peptides

Maintain the cold chain using an insulated cooler with ice packs. Peptides can tolerate room temperature for 24–48 hours occasionally. Pack in carry-on luggage for air travel.
