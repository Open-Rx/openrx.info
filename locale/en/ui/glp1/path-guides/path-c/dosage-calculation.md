## Manual Dosage Calculation Guide

> ⚠️ **Accurate math is non-negotiable.** A misplaced decimal can deliver ten or even one hundred times the intended dose and may be life-threatening. Always double-check calculations. If anything is unclear, **do not inject**.

### Why Learn Manual Calculations?

Understanding the formulas lets you verify calculator results, calculate without internet access, and catch potential errors.

### The Three-Step Manual Calculation Process

**Step 1: Find the concentration of your mixed solution**

```
Concentration (mg/mL) = Total Peptide (mg) ÷ BAC Water Added (mL)
```

*Example:* Vial contains 10 mg. BAC water added = 2 mL. Concentration = 10 mg ÷ 2 mL = **5 mg/mL**

Because 1 mg = 1000 mcg, 5 mg/mL also equals 5000 mcg/mL.

**Step 2: Work out the volume (mL) for your target dose**

```
Injection Volume (mL) = Desired Dose (mg) ÷ Concentration (mg/mL)
```

*Example:* Desired dose = 2.5 mg. Concentration = 5 mg/mL. Injection Volume = 2.5 ÷ 5 = **0.5 mL**

**Step 3: Convert mL to insulin-syringe Units**

U-100 insulin syringes hold 100 Units per mL.

```
Injection Volume (Units) = Injection Volume (mL) × 100
```

*Example:* 0.5 mL × 100 = **50 Units** — draw to the 50-Unit mark on a U-100 syringe.

### Verification Checklist

- ✅ **Units check:** Ensure the final answer is in "units," not "mL"
- ✅ **Reasonableness check:** Most doses require 5–100 units
- ✅ **Double calculate:** Do the math twice, preferably with different methods
- 🚩 **Red flag:** Needing more than 100 units (requires multiple syringes or a larger syringe)
- 🚩 **Red flag:** Less than 5 units (very hard to measure accurately)
