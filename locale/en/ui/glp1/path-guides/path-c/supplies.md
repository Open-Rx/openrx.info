## Supplies Needed & Sourcing (Path C)

1. **Lyophilized Peptide Vial** — Contains freeze-dried powder (often listed as 5 mg or 10 mg). The stated content and purity are unverified because it comes from online "research" vendors.

2. **Bacteriostatic Water** — Sterile water for injection with 0.9 percent benzyl alcohol as preservative. Essential for multi-dose storage because it slows bacterial growth after mixing. Buy only from reputable medical-supply vendors. Never use tap water, bottled water, or any other non-sterile liquid. Plain sterile water (no preservative) is acceptable only if you use the entire vial immediately after reconstitution.

3. **Mixing Syringe & Needle** — A larger sterile syringe (3 mL or 5 mL) paired with a sterile needle (18 G–23 G) for drawing BAC water and puncturing vial stoppers. Use a brand-new set for every reconstitution.

4. **Injection Syringes/Needles** — Sterile U-100 insulin syringes with fine, permanently attached needles (29 G–32 G, 5/16-inch or ½-inch) for subcutaneous injection. Common barrel sizes: 1 mL, 0.5 mL, or 0.3 mL. Markings must be clear for accurate dosing. Do not reuse syringes or needles. One fresh syringe per injection.

5. **Alcohol Swabs** — Individually wrapped, sterile 70 percent isopropyl alcohol pads for cleaning vial stoppers and skin.

6. **Sharps Container** — FDA-cleared, puncture-resistant container for every used needle and syringe (mixing and injection).

> ⚠️ Using non-sterile or counterfeit supplies increases infection risk. Always prioritize quality and sterility.

Powder comes from high-risk "research" vendors. Buy ancillary supplies (BAC water, syringes, needles, swabs, sharps container) from established medical-supply stores or local pharmacies. See [Sourcing Ancillary Supplies](/glp-1/sourcing/ancillary-supplies).
