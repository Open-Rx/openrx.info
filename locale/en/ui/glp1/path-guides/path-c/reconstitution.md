## Reconstitution (Mixing) Step-by-Step Guide

Reconstitution is the process of turning your lyophilized (freeze-dried) peptide powder into an injectable liquid. The powder must be dissolved in bacteriostatic water to create a solution with a specific concentration (mg/mL), from which you draw measured doses.

> ⚠️ **Meticulous sterile technique is critical.** Losing sterility at any point can introduce bacteria and cause serious infection.

### Step 1: Prepare

Wash hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, then dry. Set up a clean, flat surface. Lay out all supplies: peptide vial, BAC water vial, mixing syringe and needle, alcohol swabs, and a sharps container. If either vial was refrigerated, let it reach room temperature first.

### Step 2: Clean vial tops

Snap off the plastic caps from both vials. Using a new alcohol swab for each, scrub the rubber stopper for at least 10 seconds. Let the alcohol evaporate. Do not touch the stoppers after cleaning.

### Step 3: Draw BAC water

Decide on your water volume (example: a 10 mg vial mixed to 5 mg/mL needs 2 mL of BAC water). Pull air equal to that volume into the mixing syringe, insert the needle into the BAC water stopper, inject the air, invert, and draw the measured volume.

### Step 4: Add BAC water to peptide vial

Insert the needle through the center of the peptide-vial stopper. **Keep the needle bevel against the inside glass wall.** Slowly depress the plunger so the liquid streams down the wall rather than splashing on the powder cake. This reduces foaming and protects the peptide.

### Step 5: Mix the solution

Withdraw the needle and dispose of the syringe immediately. Gently swirl in small circles or roll the vial between your palms until the powder dissolves and the solution looks clear.

> **Do not shake.** Vigorous shaking creates foam and can denature the peptide.

### Step 6: Inspect the solution

Hold the vial up to a light. The solution must be crystal-clear with no particles, cloudiness, or color change. If anything looks off, discard the vial safely — it may be contaminated or inadequately dissolved.

### Step 7: Label the reconstituted vial

Write on the vial:
- Date and time of reconstitution
- Final concentration (e.g., "5 mg/mL")
- Calculated expiration date (commonly 28 days refrigerated)
