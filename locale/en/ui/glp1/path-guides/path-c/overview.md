## Using Lyophilized Powder (DIY Peptides) — ⚠️ HIGH RISK

This is the most complex and dangerous option in this guide. Freeze-dried peptide powder is sourced from online research chemical vendors. Labels state products are "for research use only" and "not for human consumption."

**Before proceeding, you must:**
- Understand and accept the significant dangers
- Conduct extensive independent research
- Commit to following strict sterile technique
- Source supplies from reputable medical outlets
- Accurately perform reconstitution and dosage calculations
- Acknowledge that you are operating outside regulated medical practice

### Specific Risks and Considerations

**Source reliability and product quality:** Identity and purity are unknown. The powder may contain the wrong peptide, be underdosed, or contain contaminants. Serious infections may follow from bacterial or endotoxin contamination. Vendor certificates are often unreliable; third-party testing costs around $300 per sample.

**Sterility during handling:** Bacteriostatic water cannot sterilize an already-contaminated vial.

**Calculation errors:** A single decimal error can create a ten-fold or hundred-fold overdose.

**Legal and ethical issues:** Purchasing research-labeled products may violate local laws.

**Lack of medical oversight:** You assume sole responsibility for monitoring your health.

### Retatrutide Considerations

Retatrutide has fewer reliable sources, higher potency requiring precise dosing, and Janoshik testing is strongly recommended. Typically available in 10 mg vials. Standard refrigeration required.
