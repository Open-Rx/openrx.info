## Understanding Your Options: Pen/Tablet vs. Vial vs. Powder

This section outlines the three main approaches available through gray-market channels. Understanding the characteristics, requirements, and risks of each path is essential before you proceed.

### Path A · Ready-to-Use (Pens, Pre-Filled Syringes, Tablets)

Ready-to-use products need almost no preparation and are often imported through gray-market channels.

- **Single-Use Injector Pens:** The "Tizaro" pen (tirzepatide) from Ziska Pharmaceuticals, shipped via India, delivers one weekly dose per pen. Operation is straightforward, but the supply chain is not formally regulated.
- **Pre-filled Syringes:** The "Semaglo OB" pre-filled syringe (semaglutide) from Ziska Pharmaceuticals contains a single weekly dose that is ready for immediate injection.
- **Oral Tablets:** Rybelsus® tablets (semaglutide) from Novo Nordisk are legally approved but often imported through gray-market suppliers to lower costs or bypass access barriers.

**Who is this for?**
- People wanting the simplest setup with minimal preparation
- Users willing to handle gray-market shipping uncertainties
- Those preferring to avoid mixing or measuring doses

### Path B · Authentic Vial (Mounjaro® Tirzepatide)

Indian-market Mounjaro® vials are manufactured by Eli Lilly and use the same serialized packaging as US products. You can confirm authenticity with `scan.lilly.com`. Vials are available only in 2.5 mg and 5 mg strengths. Each vial is single use — draw the entire contents into a syringe and inject once.

**Who is this for?**
- People seeking authentic branded medication from the gray market
- Those wanting lower prices than domestic pharmacies
- Users willing to manage international shipping

### Path C · DIY Powder (Lyophilized Peptides) ⚠️ HIGH RISK

This route buys semaglutide, tirzepatide, or retatrutide as freeze-dried powder from online "research chemical" vendors. Labels state "for research use only" and "not for human use."

**Technical steps required:** Reconstitution (mixing with bacteriostatic water), dosage calculation, and subcutaneous injection using an insulin syringe.

Per-dose cost is lowest, but hazards are highest — unknown purity, sterility, and potency, plus a much larger chance of user error. This path is dangerous and should only be chosen by those who have thoroughly researched the risks.

**Who is this for?**
- People seeking the lowest possible cost with serious risk tolerance
- Users prepared to research, calculate, and follow strict sterile technique
