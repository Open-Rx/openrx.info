### How Each Medication Works

**Semaglutide (GLP-1 Only)** mimics the GLP-1 hormone. It slows gastric emptying, reduces appetite, and improves insulin sensitivity. This is the most established option with years of safety data.

**Tirzepatide (Dual Agonist)** activates both GLP-1 and GIP receptors, providing enhanced glucose control, greater appetite suppression, and improved metabolic effects compared to semaglutide alone.

**Retatrutide (Triple Agonist)** activates GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon receptors. Maximum metabolic activation, increased energy expenditure, and the most comprehensive appetite control — but the least safety data of the three.

### Which One Should You Choose?

**Consider Semaglutide if:** You're new to GLP-1 medications, want the most established option, prefer maximum vendor choice, or are sensitive to side effects.

**Consider Tirzepatide if:** You want enhanced weight loss over semaglutide, have plateaued on semaglutide, want good availability with better efficacy, or can handle slightly more complex dosing.

**Consider Retatrutide if:** You want maximum weight loss potential, are comfortable with newer medications, can source from verified vendors, are willing to pay premium prices, and are comfortable with potential for more side effects.

> Remember: The "best" medication is the one you can access reliably, tolerate well, and use consistently.
