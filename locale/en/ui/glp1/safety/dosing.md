## Understanding Dosing Principles: Start Low, Go Slow

GLP-1 RA therapy follows one overriding rule: **start low, go slow.** Beginning with minimal weekly doses allows your body to adapt and minimizes common side effects.

### Tirzepatide Protocol

| Period | Dose |
|--------|------|
| Weeks 1–4 (initiation) | 2.5 mg weekly subcutaneously |
| After week 4 | Increase to 5 mg |
| Maximum (Mounjaro®) | Up to 15 mg weekly |

### Semaglutide Protocol

| Period | Dose |
|--------|------|
| Weeks 1–4 (initiation) | 0.25 mg weekly |
| After week 4 | Increase to 0.5 mg |
| Maximum (Ozempic®) | 2 mg weekly |
| Maximum (Wegovy®) | 2.4 mg weekly |

### Retatrutide Protocol

| Period | Dose |
|--------|------|
| Initiation | 1–2 mg weekly (trial-based extrapolation) |
| Titration | Increase 1–2 mg every 4 weeks |
| Therapeutic range | 5–15 mg weekly |
| Gray market maximum available | Up to 30 mg weekly |

> ⚠️ **Critical warning:** Doses above 15 mg exceed those tested in published clinical trials. Triple receptor activation risks include pronounced GI effects, increased heart rate, hypoglycemia risk, and unknown long-term effects.

### Consistency

Inject on the same day each week to maintain steady drug levels. If side effects emerge, consider holding at your current dose for an extra 4 weeks before titrating up, or reducing to the previous dose.

> Only a qualified healthcare provider should decide when to start, raise, hold, or lower your dose.
