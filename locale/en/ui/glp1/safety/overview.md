## Universal Safety Principles: Overview & General Injection Safety

This section provides core safety guidance for **all** GLP-1 RA users, no matter how the medication is sourced or administered (except oral tablets). Master these basics before attempting any injection.

### General Injection Safety Concepts

**1. Cleanliness (Aseptic Technique)**

Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water before beginning. Use a brand-new sterile needle and syringe for each shot. Wipe both the vial stopper and your skin with separate alcohol swabs and let each surface air-dry before puncturing or injecting.

**2. Injection-Site Rotation**

Do not inject into the exact same spot repeatedly. Rotating between areas (abdomen, thighs, upper arms) prevents lumps such as lipohypertrophy and promotes reliable absorption. Divide large areas into smaller sections, maintain 1–2 inch spacing from previous injections, rotate between general regions weekly, and keep a log or calendar. Avoid moles, scars, stretch marks, bruises, and any skin that is red, broken, infected, or hardened.

**3. Safe Sharps Disposal**

Place every used needle, syringe, or single-use pen directly into an approved puncture-resistant sharps container immediately after use. Never toss loose sharps into household trash or recycling.

### Absolute Rule: No Sharing Devices or Needles

**Never share injection pens, vials, syringes, or needles — even if you swap the needle.**

Sharing any of this equipment can transmit blood-borne infections such as HIV and hepatitis B or C. The rule applies to Paths A, B, and C. Each person must have their own dedicated set.
