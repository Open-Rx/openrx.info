## Sending to the Vendor's Wallet Address — CRITICAL

> ⚠️ **This is the step where funds are most often lost forever.** After a transaction is confirmed on its blockchain, it cannot be cancelled, reversed, or retrieved. Sending to the wrong address or on the wrong network almost always results in permanent loss of funds.

### Get the Exact Address and Network from Your Vendor

The vendor must provide a precise wallet address. Bitcoin addresses only work for BTC. Ethereum addresses handle ETH or ERC-20 tokens — but NOT Tron (TRC-20) tokens. An ERC-20 transfer to a TRC-20 address will disappear. If the vendor provides a QR code, scan it but confirm the text address and network before sending.

### Double-Check Everything Before You Click "Send"

1. **Copy and paste** — never type the address manually
2. **Match the first four and last four characters** of the pasted address against what the vendor provided
3. **Confirm the cryptocurrency** — are you sending BTC, USDT, USDC, etc.?
4. **Confirm the network** — e.g., Ethereum (ERC-20), Tron (TRC-20), Solana
5. **Confirm the address again** — one final check
6. **Review fees and net amount** — make sure you're sending the right amount after fees
7. **Optional: send a small test amount first** — then confirm receipt before sending the full payment
