## Safe Disposal of Sharps & Medication

Throwing used needles or expired drugs in household trash puts family, sanitation workers, and the environment at risk. Follow these guidelines every time.

### Sharps Disposal (Needles, Syringes, Pens)

"Sharps" means any used needle, syringe (mixing or injection), or single-use pen.

> ⚠️ **NEVER recap a used needle.** Most accidental needlesticks happen during recapping.

**Five-step process:**

1. **Immediate disposal:** Drop the entire used item straight into an FDA-cleared sharps container as soon as you finish the injection or mixing step.
2. **Choose the right container:** Use only a puncture-resistant plastic container with a tight, leak-proof lid labeled for sharps.
3. **Do not overfill:** Stop using the container when it reaches about ¾ full.
4. **Seal it:** Close the lid securely (tape it if instructed).
5. **Final disposal:** Do not place the sealed container in household trash or recycling unless your local program allows it. Contact your local waste authority, health department, or pharmacy. In the United States, SafeNeedleDisposal.org lists state-specific disposal options including drop-off boxes, hazardous-waste collection sites, mail-back programs, and special pick-up services.

### Medication Disposal (Vials & Tablets)

**Used single-dose vials:** After withdrawing the dose, place the empty vial in household trash. Peel off or black out any personal details first.

**Expired or unused reconstituted peptide vials:** Discard in household trash after removing labels.

**Unused or expired tablets:** Do not flush unless a reliable source specifically permits it. Prefer pharmacy or community take-back programs. If none are available: mix tablets (whole, not crushed) with an unpalatable substance such as used coffee grounds, dirt, or cat litter. Seal the mixture in a plastic bag. Put the sealed container in household trash. Remove or obscure personal information on the empty bottle.
