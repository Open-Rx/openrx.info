## Safe Disposal

Used needles don't go in the trash or recycling. They can puncture through bags, injure sanitation workers, and end up in places they shouldn't. The same goes for expired medication.

### Needles, syringes, and pens

As soon as you finish an injection or a mixing step, drop the needle or syringe directly into a sharps container. Don't recap it first — most accidental needlesticks happen during recapping.

A sharps container is just a puncture-resistant plastic container with a tight lid. You can get them at most pharmacies for a few dollars, or sometimes free through local programs. Fill it to about three-quarters full, then seal it and stop using it.

For disposal, don't put the sealed container in household trash unless your local program explicitly allows it. Options vary by area: pharmacies, hospital drop-offs, hazardous waste collection days, or mail-back programs. In the US, SafeNeedleDisposal.org has a state-by-state list.

### Vials and medication

Empty single-use vials can go in household trash. Scratch out or cover any personal information on the label first.

Unused or expired reconstituted peptides go in household trash as well, after removing labels.

For unused tablets, check whether there's a pharmacy take-back program nearby first. If not: mix the tablets with something unpleasant like used coffee grounds, dirt, or cat litter, seal it in a bag, and put it in the trash. Don't flush medication unless a reliable source says it's safe for that specific drug.
