## Subcutaneous Injection Technique

A subcutaneous (subq) injection goes into the fat layer just under the skin. It's the standard route for GLP-1 medications and most HRT formulations that are injected.

### Where to inject

You want somewhere with a decent layer of fat. The abdomen works well for most people (stay at least 2 inches from the navel). The front or outer thigh is another good option. The back of the upper arm is usable but harder to reach on your own.

Skip anything that's bony, bruised, scarred, or has stretch marks. Hardened or lumpy tissue from previous injections is also off-limits until it recovers.

### Rotating sites

Injecting the same spot over and over creates lipohypertrophy, which is basically scar tissue that absorbs medication inconsistently. It sounds annoying to track but it genuinely matters.

Pick a rotation pattern that's easy to remember. A lot of people divide their abdomen into quadrants and move clockwise, or alternate between abdomen and thigh week to week. Whatever you do, try to stay at least an inch away from wherever you last injected.

### How to inject

**Wash your hands first.** Get everything ready before you start: syringe, medication, alcohol swabs, sharps container within reach.

Wipe the injection site with an alcohol swab and let it fully dry before proceeding. Injecting through wet alcohol stings more and isn't good practice.

If the site doesn't have much fat on it, pinch a fold of skin to lift it away from the muscle underneath.

Hold the syringe like you'd hold a dart. For most people and most sites, you're going in at 90 degrees. Only go to 45 degrees if you have very little subcutaneous fat and a longer needle, otherwise you risk going into muscle.

Insert quickly, which actually hurts less than a slow hesitant insertion. Then push the plunger down slowly and steadily until it's empty.

Hold the needle in place for about 5-10 seconds before pulling out. This cuts down on leakback. Withdraw at the same angle you went in.

If there's a small drop of blood, light pressure with a cotton ball or gauze is fine. Don't rub it.

Drop the syringe straight into your sharps container. Don't recap the needle.
