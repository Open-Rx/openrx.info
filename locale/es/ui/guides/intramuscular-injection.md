## Intramuscular Injection Technique

An intramuscular (IM) injection goes into muscle tissue rather than the fat layer under the skin. It absorbs faster than subcutaneous and is used for some HRT formulations, particularly oil-based estradiol valerate, estradiol cypionate, and testosterone.

### Where to inject

**Ventrogluteal** is the preferred site for most people. It's on the side of the hip. Find the greater trochanter (the bony bump on the outer hip), put your palm over it, and inject into the triangle formed between your spread index and middle fingers pointing toward your waist. This area has relatively few major blood vessels and nerves compared to other IM sites.

**Vastus lateralis** (outer thigh) is the outer third of the thigh, roughly midway between knee and hip. It's easier to reach on your own and fine for smaller volumes.

**Deltoid** (upper arm) works for small volumes only, 1 mL or less. It's difficult to self-inject and not ideal for regular use.

Worth noting: the dorsogluteal site (upper buttock) gets mentioned in older guides but it's close to the sciatic nerve and generally worth avoiding.

### Needle selection

You need a longer needle than for subcutaneous injections. A 25 G x 1 inch needle is standard for injection; 1.5 inch if you have more tissue to get through. Use a separate drawing needle (21-22 G x 1.5 inch) to pull the medication into the syringe, then swap to the fresh injection needle before you inject. The drawing needle gets dulled by the vial stopper, and a dull needle is more painful.

### How to inject

Wash your hands. Draw your dose with the drawing needle, then swap to the injection needle. If the medication was in the fridge, let it warm up for a bit first. Cold oil is thick and injecting it hurts significantly more.

Wipe the injection site with an alcohol swab and let it dry.

Use the Z-track method: pull the skin about an inch to the side before you insert the needle. Hold it there through the whole injection. When you release after withdrawing, the tissue shifts back and seals the needle track, which stops oil from leaking back up.

Insert at 90 degrees, quickly and smoothly.

Some older guides tell you to aspirate (pull the plunger back to check for blood before injecting). Current guidelines generally don't require it for standard IM sites, but it's your call.

Inject slowly, around 10 seconds per mL. Going fast builds pressure in the muscle and it hurts.

Hold for 10 seconds, release the skin stretch, then withdraw at the same angle.

Gentle pressure after is fine. A slow massage of the site can help distribute oil-based medications. Both the drawing needle and the injection needle go in the sharps container.
